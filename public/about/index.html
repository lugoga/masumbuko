About

Masumbuko Semba is data scientist works at the Nelson Mandela African Institution of Science and Technology and doing a doctoral degree in physical oceanogrphy at the University of Dar es Salaam. He specializes in Earth Observations, where he use open-source geo-spatial tools and satellite observations to understand the physical processes that cause changes in our environments. Among the project he is involved is Global Drifter Program, where he works with the Physical Oceanographic Division of the Atlantic Oceanographic and Meteorological Laboratory (AOML) of National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) to understnd the ocean circulation in the Tropical Indian Ocean region. What we do in this initiative is to combine satellite altimetry and drifter observations to track pathways of surface current.
